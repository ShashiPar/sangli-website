Top Spots to Visit in Sangli

Ganapati Temple

The Sangli Ganapati Temple, located 4 km from Sangli Railway Station and 47 km from Kolhapur, is a prominent Hindu shrine dedicated to Lord Ganesh. Established by Chintamanrao Patwardhan in 1814 and completed in 1845, the temple is situated on the eastern banks of the Krishna River. Known for its Peshwa architecture, the temple features black stone construction and intricately carved multi-colored wooden doors. The temple complex includes a large hall, a platform, and a 'Nagarkhana,' and houses a marble idol of Ganesha with Riddhi-siddhi. Surrounding the main temple are four smaller temples dedicated to Chintamaneshwar, Chintamaneshwari, Suryanarayana, and Laxminarayan, collectively known as Ganapati Panchayat. Ganeshotsav is celebrated annually for five days with various cultural programs.

Dandoba Hills Forest Reserve

The Dandoba Hills Forest Reserve is a pristine sanctuary teeming with diverse flora and fauna, offering a haven for nature enthusiasts. This untouched wilderness also harbors several historical landmarks, including the revered Shiva temple, which stands as the holiest local shrine. Regularly visited by commuters and locals for prayer, the temple adds a spiritual dimension to the natural beauty of the forest. With its serene trails and panoramic views, Dandoba Hills provides a perfect escape for those seeking adventure, tranquility, and a connection to both nature and history.

Krishna Ghat

Krishna Ghat in Sangli is a picturesque and culturally significant spot situated on the banks of the Krishna River. This serene ghat is known for its scenic beauty and tranquil ambiance, making it a popular destination for both locals and visitors. The ghat is adorned with ancient temples and ghats that are used for religious rituals, ceremonies, and daily prayers. One of the most notable features of Krishna Ghat is its connection to various festivals and events, especially during the auspicious occasions when devotees gather to take a holy dip in the river. The area around Krishna Ghat is also a hub for social and cultural activities, where people come together to celebrate local traditions and enjoy the serene environment. With its blend of natural beauty, spiritual significance, and cultural vibrancy, Krishna Ghat stands out as a must-visit location in Sangli.
