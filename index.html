Welcome to Sangli

आमची सांगली खूप चांगली!

Sangli, the "Turmeric City of India," is a vibrant metropolitan hub in Maharashtra. Located on the banks of the Krishna River, it is renowned for its vast turmeric trade, sugar factories, and Asia's largest raisin market. Together with its twin city Miraj, Sangli forms a major healthcare center with over 1000 hospitals and clinics. Discover the charm and industry of one of Maharashtra's largest cities.

History of Sangli

Sangli has a rich historical background, with roots tracing back to ancient times. It has been a significant center for trade and culture in Maharashtra.

Historical Formation and Significance

On August 1, 1949, South Satara district, comprising six talukas from Satara and two from Karnataka, was established. It included villages under Jat, Aundh, Kurundwad, Miraj, and Sangli institutions. On November 21, 1960, post the formation of United Maharashtra, it was renamed Sangli district. In 1965, two new talukas, Kavthe Mahankal and Atpadi, were created. By 1999, Palus taluka was formed, followed by Kadgaon on March 28, 2002. Sangli's Ganpati Temple is privately maintained by Shrimantraje. This district, the birthplace of Marathi theater, hosted the first Marathi drama, Sita Swaynwar, presented by Vishnudas Bhave.

Cultural and Artistic Heritage

Sangli is a cradle of Marathi culture and theater. Vithojirao Chavan, a hero who challenged Aurangzeb, and Pranate Nana Patil, an opposition figure, hail from here. It is renowned for fine-quality fiber production and as the birthplace of Vasantdada Patil, a prominent organization leader. Narayan Shreepad Rajhans, aka Balgandharva, a celebrated early 20th-century Marathi theater actor and singer, gained fame for his female roles and influence on singing styles like Khayal and Thumri. His career was honored by Bal Gangadhar Tilak, enhancing his legacy.

Industrial and Agricultural Progress

Sangli district boasts numerous sugar factories, including the Vasantdada Patil Shetkari Sahakari Sugar Factory, the top co-operative sugar factory in Asia. The region is advancing in industrial and agricultural sectors, alongside its rich artistic heritage. Sangli experienced the reign of Maurya, Satvahan, Wakataka, Rashtrakut, Yadav, and Bahamani dynasties. The Patwardhan family ruled it during the Peshwa era. A significant satyagraha took place in Bilasi, Shirala taluka, during the pre-independence civil disobedience movement. Miraj is famed for its globally exported vessels and the Nagpanchami celebration started in 32 Shirala by Gorakhnath Maharaj
